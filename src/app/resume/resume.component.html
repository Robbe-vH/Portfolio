Who am I

I would like to describe myself as a person who is eager to learn and develop his skills. During my free time, I spend a lot of time on Pluralsight learning new skills to add to my set. As a kid, I used to love experimenting with electronics, disassembeling old amplifiers, RC cars and planes and making them into new Frankenstein devices. When I got my first job, most of my money was spend on hardware for building a PC and that's where my IT journey started. I got hands on experience with making a hackingtosh, swapping every component, installing Linux, crashing the system a couple of times and fixing it. At the time it felt like having a new world opening, the possibilities where endless. Now those years pay off in terms of the experience on system administration. There is still much to learn in the system domain, but of course my main interest is programming. I started my journey in 5th grade back in high school, the usual introduction to web development, Java, OOP in Visual Basic, etc. After completing two years of high school, I dropped out for a non IT-related job which I thought would be giving me enough sattisfaction, but after a couple of years, I felt like I missed programming too much and I enrolled in a undergraduate degree in Programming at PXL.
