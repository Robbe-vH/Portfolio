At Thai Cafe, I started out driving around delivering TakeAway orders. After a couple of months though my employer asked me if I would be interested in helping behind the bar and serving plates to the tables. I very much enjoy helping where I can and having contact with the customers.

I applied for a single-month student job in the summer, but due to a shortage of mailmen, my employer asked me if I could stay for four months. It was a pretty intense work schedule with a lot of pressure and not a lot of guidance. It took me a couple of weeks to get used to the job and find the right ways of getting all letters and packages delivered within my paid time, but once I got the hang of it, the job turned out to be pretty fun in its own way.

For my years at Pauwels, I am grateful for all the learning experiences my employer and colleagues gave me. Not only did I learn to work the mechanics of bikes, but first and foremost I got hands-on experience talking to customers and honing my social skills. Due to my moving to Hasselt, I had to give up this student job, but it will always be an important part of my early work experience.

This was one of the more random jobs I got into. A friend of mine asked me if I would be interested in helping him out with a summer camp for children with disabilities. At first I didn't feel comfortable with the idea because I felt like I could do more harm than good, but he assured me I would be okay. After getting to know the children and their little quirks, I had an awesome time caring for and playing with them.

Coming from my interest in mechanics, I got a position as a kart mechanic at my local karting circuit during summer vacation. Even though it was pretty fun working there, I chose my job at the bicycle shop when school started again.<
